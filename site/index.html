What is spaced repetition?
A scheduling method that resurfaces information at expanding intervals to strengthen long‑term memory.

Why This Works

1. Active Recall

You try to retrieve the answer before seeing it. This struggle signals the brain to reinforce pathways.

2. Self‑Assessment

A quick rating (gesture or click) captures subjective difficulty – a powerful proxy for future forgetting.

3. Adaptive Spacing

Easier items recede. Hard or uncertain ones resurface sooner. Time is focused where retention is weakest.

4. Reduced Friction

Hands‑free rating keeps cognitive focus on material – not UI clicks – preserving attention and flow.

Memory Cycle

1Attempt

Recall before reveal.

2Reveal

Expose answer; compare.

3Rate

Gesture or chip = difficulty.

4Schedule

Engine updates next interval.

5Reinforce

Timely re‑exposure cements.

Each loop optimizes spacing: early density → widening gaps → durable memory.
